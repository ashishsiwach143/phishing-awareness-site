What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where criminals impersonate legitimate organizations or individuals to trick you into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

The term "phishing" comes from the analogy of fishing - attackers cast out "bait" (fake emails, messages, or websites) hoping victims will "bite" by clicking malicious links or providing confidential information.

Types of Phishing Attacks

üìß

Email Phishing

The most common type. Attackers send fraudulent emails that appear to be from trusted sources like banks, companies, or government agencies.

Example:

"Your account has been suspended. Click here to verify your identity within 24 hours or lose access permanently."

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks aimed at specific individuals or organizations. Attackers research their victims to make messages more convincing.

Example:

"Hi John, I saw your presentation at the conference last week. Here's the document you requested about Project Phoenix."

üì±

Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Phishing attacks delivered via text messages. Often claim to be from delivery services, banks, or government agencies.

Example:

"USPS: Your package is pending. Confirm delivery address: bit.ly/pkgXYZ123"

üìû

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Phone-based attacks where scammers impersonate legitimate organizations to extract information or money.

Example:

"This is Microsoft Tech Support. We've detected viruses on your computer. We need remote access to fix it immediately."

Whaling

High-profile attacks targeting senior executives, CEOs, or other "big fish" in organizations for financial fraud.

Example:

"CEO to CFO: I'm in an urgent meeting. Wire $50,000 to this vendor account immediately. I'll explain later."

Clone Phishing

Attackers create near-identical copies of legitimate emails you've received before, but replace links or attachments with malicious ones.

Example:

"Resending the invoice from last week - please review the updated version attached."
